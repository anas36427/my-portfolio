Overview

CFix is a digital platform designed to streamline hostel-related processes at Aligarh Muslim University (AMU). The system replaces traditional manual workflows for complaints, hostel applications, and communication, creating a smoother, more transparent, and user-friendly experience.

Problem Space

Through user interviews and process analysis (students, wardens, hostel administration):

  • • Complaints were handled manually → slow updates & no tracking.
  • • Hostel forms like leave, room change, and medical applications were on paper → prone to loss & delays.
  • • No centralized dashboard for wardens/admins → difficult to manage workload.
  • • Students had no visibility into status → constant follow-ups.
  • • No audit trail or proper documentation for record keeping.

Target Users

  • Students (primary users)
  • Wardens (complaint handlers)
  • Provost / DSW (escalation & approvals)
  • IT Staff (system maintenance)

Key Features

  • Role-Based Dashboards
  • Smart Complaint Submission
  • Online Application Forms
  • Real-Time Notifications

Design Process & UI

I began with grayscale wireframes focusing on form clarity, clear hierarchy, and minimal cognitive load. The high-fidelity design uses color-coding for statuses (Pending, Processing, Resolved), card-based dashboards, and WCAG 2.1 compliant typography.

Dashboard UI

Outcome & Impact

From pilot testing: Students reported less confusion & fewer follow-ups. Staff experienced faster resolution workflows. ~1.5 second average response time achieved. Centralized records improved auditing & administration.
